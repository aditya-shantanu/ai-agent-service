Why $/agent falls with scale — then stops falling

$/agent/month vs fleet size (log scale), computed from the fields on the left

The mechanics: total cost = fixed + variable. Fixed (cluster fee, system pool, warm spares) is the same at 10 agents or 10,000 — per agent it shrinks as 1/N, which is the steep left side of the curve. Variable (each agent's duty-cycle share of a pod slot + its always-on disk) is per-agent by nature — it never shrinks. Once fixed costs are spread thin (~10k agents), only the variable part remains: the curve flattens at the at-scale floor shown in the tile above. Past that point, scale adds capacity linearly but no longer adds discount — further savings must come from shrinking the variable part itself (duty cycle, slot price, disk).
